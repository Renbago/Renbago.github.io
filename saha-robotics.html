Work at Saha Robotik

Saha Robotik is a technology company developing autonomous waiter, cargo, and guide robots. I work full-time on the localization, mapping, navigation, and control systems used by robots operating continuously in commercial environments.

  • Lead the development and maintenance of localization and mapping systems.
  • Work with ROS 2, C++, and Python across the production robot software.
  • Maintain navigation and control systems used by more than 400 deployed robots operating 24/7.
  • Developed 2D LiDAR obstacle detection for dynamic and static objects, including trajectory estimation.
  • Managed the localization and mapping lifecycle of the Istanbul Airport autonomous guide-robot project.
  • Currently working on OpenArm-based mobile manipulation, robot data collection, and VLA policies.

My previous portfolio page recorded 150+ active robots in 2025; the current portfolio figure has since grown beyond 200.

Istanbul Airport

I led the localization and mapping work for Saha's autonomous guide robot at Istanbul Airport. The system operates 24/7, travels at least 25 km per day, and uses a map covering 187,514 m². My work included producing and maintaining the map, localization and SLAM tuning, navigation integration, field testing, and follow-up improvements based on live operation.

Azerbaijan ROS Training Program

From February to April 2025, I led Saha Robotik's ROS training program for university students in Azerbaijan. I first taught the group remotely for one month, then traveled to Azerbaijan for the in-person phase. During the visit I delivered practical ROS sessions, attended the students' final presentations, and evaluated the projects they had developed from the training.
