Work at Saha Robotik

Saha Robotik is a technology company developing autonomous waiter, cargo, and guide robots. I work full-time on the localization, mapping, navigation, and control systems used by robots operating continuously in commercial environments.

  • Lead the development and maintenance of localization and mapping systems.
  • Work with ROS 2, C++, and Python across the production robot software.
  • Helped develop and continue to maintain the robot-control stack used by more than 400 deployed robots operating 24/7.
  • Developed multiple localization and mapping algorithms for deployed systems; further implementation details are confidential.
  • Developed 2D LiDAR obstacle detection for dynamic and static objects, including trajectory estimation.
  • Lead the Istanbul Airport guide-robot project, including planning, mapping, localization, field testing, and deployment.
  • Currently working on OpenArm-based mobile manipulation, robot data collection, and VLA policies.

Istanbul Airport

To the best of my knowledge, the 187,514 m² map used for Saha Robotik's Istanbul Airport project is the largest publicly shared indoor map used for an autonomous service-robot deployment. After a passenger scans a boarding pass, the robot identifies the destination gate and autonomously guides the passenger there.

The project had been planned since 2019. After I joined Saha Robotik in October 2023, I took responsibility for bringing it into operation in 2024. Over a four-month period, I planned the rollout, created and validated the 187,514 m² airport map, prepared the localization system, organized the full field-test sequence, integrated the navigation workflow, and commissioned the first robot. The deployed robot operates 24/7 and travels at least 25 km per day.

After the first deployment, I contributed to commissioning more than five additional robots through the continuing airport agreements. I still lead the technical side of this project alongside my other responsibilities at Saha Robotik.

Azerbaijan ROS Training Program

From February to April 2025, I led Saha Robotik's ROS training program for university students in Azerbaijan. I first taught the group remotely for one month, then traveled to Azerbaijan for the in-person phase. During the visit I delivered practical ROS sessions, attended the students' final presentations, and evaluated the projects they had developed from the training.
